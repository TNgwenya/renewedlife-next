# Phases Completed So Far

## 1. Discovery and Benchmarking — Completed
We researched strong church websites and identified website patterns that matter most for Renewed Life:
- clear homepage messaging
- strong first-time visitor journey
- visible service times and location
- modern mobile-first design
- sermon and online ministry access
- clear giving and connect pathways

### Main outcome
A benchmark-driven direction was chosen rather than redesigning blindly.

## 2. Review of Current Website — Completed
We reviewed the current Renewed Life website and identified the main issues.

### Key findings
- The site contains the right basic content categories, but the user journey is weak.
- The homepage has repeated calls to action and does not lead the visitor clearly.
- Some language feels outdated and creates friction.
- The current visual presentation feels template-based rather than premium and intentional.
- The site does not yet communicate “what to expect” strongly enough for first-time visitors.
- There is no strong dedicated **Plan Your Visit** journey.
- Important trust signals can be improved: photos, testimonies, clearer ministry pathways, stronger contact visibility.

## 3. Strategic Direction Selection — Completed
We selected a practical benchmark direction for Renewed Life.

### Decision
We are not trying to copy a mega-church website feature-for-feature.  
We are building a site that is:
- modern
- realistic
- conversion-focused
- welcoming
- maintainable

### Primary mirrored model
**Meta Church** was chosen as the closest practical benchmark for homepage structure and visitor journey.

### Secondary borrowings
- **Life.Church** for digital ministry maturity
- **Elevation Church** for homepage clarity and next steps
- **Hillsong Church** for multi-ministry organization inspiration
- **Mariners Church** for warmth, storytelling, and content presentation
