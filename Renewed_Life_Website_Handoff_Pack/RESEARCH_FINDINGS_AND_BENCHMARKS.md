# Research Findings and Benchmark Takeaways

## The 5 benchmark websites selected
1. Life.Church
2. Elevation Church
3. Hillsong Church
4. Mariners Church
5. Meta Church

## Why Meta Church became the main mirror
Meta Church provides the strongest model for a growing church that needs:
- a simple but polished homepage
- strong calls to action
- visitor-first navigation
- a practical structure that can be achieved without a massive media team

## Core patterns discovered across strong church websites

### 1. The homepage must answer first-time visitor questions fast
Within the first few seconds, a new visitor should be able to find:
- what the church is about
- when services happen
- where the church is located
- how to plan a visit
- how to watch online

### 2. The best church sites do not overload the hero section
They normally use:
- one strong message
- one compelling image or visual
- two to four clear actions

### 3. “Plan Your Visit” is a major conversion tool
The strongest sites reduce uncertainty by showing:
- service times
- map/location
- what to expect
- children/family information
- atmosphere and dress culture
- simple contact options

### 4. Modern church sites are action-oriented
They guide the user toward:
- visit
- watch
- connect
- give
- join a ministry or group

### 5. Trust is built visually and structurally
Strong sites use:
- real photography
- clean spacing
- consistent branding
- concise copy
- warm language
- visible next steps

### 6. The site must work as a ministry platform, not only an information board
The best sites make it easy to:
- watch sermons
- contact the church
- submit interest
- access giving
- follow events
- connect with a ministry

## Main weaknesses found in the current Renewed Life site
- outdated or friction-heavy visitor flow
- repeated CTA patterns
- weak visual hierarchy
- insufficient first-time visitor reassurance
- less polished layout and typography
- limited premium visual feel
- underdeveloped online ministry pathway
